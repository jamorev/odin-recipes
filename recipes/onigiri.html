Onigiri - Pokemon

The Sandwich of the East

Japanese rice balls or, Onigiri, are a staple of Japanese lunch boxes. They are usually shaped into rounds or triangles by hand.

Ingredients

  • 1 to 2 sheets dried nori seaweed
  • 4 cups steamed Japanese rice (sushi rice)
  • Salt
  • 1 ounce black sesame seeds

optional

  • 1 umeboshi
  • 1 salmon fillet

Steps

  1. Gather the Ingredients.
  2. Cut each nori sheet into 8 or 9 strips and put about 1/2 cup of steamed rice in a rice bowl.
  3. Wet your hands with water so that rice won't stick.
  4. Rub some salt on your wet hands.
  5. Place steamed rice in your hand and form into a trianble, making sure it is dense and thick.
  6. Put your favorite filling, such umeboshi or salmon, on rice and push the filling into the rice slightly.
  7. Hold rice between palms.
  8. Form rice into a round, a triangle, or a cylinder by pressing lightly with both palms, securing filling in the middle. Roll rice ball in your hands a few times, pressing lightly.
  9. Wrap rice ball with a strip or two of nori, and sprinkle some sesame seeds on them.
